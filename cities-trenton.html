WELCOME TO TRENTON

Trenton, New Jersey, is the state capital and a historic urban city located in Mercer County, within the central region of the state. Situated along the Delaware River, Trenton serves as a cultural and economic hub, known for its rich Revolutionary War history and diverse community. As part of the broader Northeast megalopolis, the city offers a mix of urban amenities, historical landmarks, and access to nearby metropolitan areas like Philadelphia and New York City.

Early History

Trenton has a rich early history dating back to its founding in 1679 by Quaker settlers. Originally known as "Trent-towne" after William Trent, one of its leading landowners, the city was officially incorporated in 1792. Trenton played a pivotal role in the American Revolution, most notably as the site of George Washington's victory at the Battle of Trenton in 1776. Its strategic location along the Delaware River contributed to its growth as a commercial and industrial hub in the early years of the United States.

Today

Today, Trenton stands a diverse and historic city with a population of approximately 90,871 residents, serving as the state capital. While it faces economic challenges, Trenton is home to significant cultural and historical landmarks, such as the New Jersey State House, the Old Barracks Museum, and the Trenton City Museum. The city also offers recreational opportunities along the Delaware River and hosts events that celebrate its rich heritage. Efforts to revitalize its downtown and neighborhoods continue, aiming to restore Trenton's prominence as a cultural and economic hub.

COST OF LIVING

While it features some suburban neighborhoods on its outskirts, the city's dense population, infrastructure, and central location along the Delaware River firmly classify it as an urban area, further emphasized by its mix of residential, commercial, and industrial zones. Because of its variety of neighborhoods, your cost of living is greatly affected depending on where you land in the city. On the whole, however, the city is more affordable than other areas of NJ.

Compared to the Average

According to recent data from the US Census, the average household income in Trenton is approximately $47,000, which is below the state average of $101,050. The cost of living is relatively affordable compared to other parts of New Jersey, with housing costs helping to keep expenses lower, though residents may still face economic challenges as the city continues its efforts toward revitalization and growth.

LIFESTYLE

Trenton, New Jersey, offers an urban lifestyle characterized by its rich history, cultural diversity, and a mix of residential, commercial, and industrial areas. As an urban city, it features a dense population, walkable neighborhoods, and access to public transportation, making it a convenient place to live and explore.

Residents and visitors can enjoy a variety of activities, such as exploring historical landmarks like the Old Barracks Museum and the New Jersey State House, visiting the New Jersey State Museum for art and science exhibits, or strolling along the Delaware River for outdoor recreation. The city also hosts community events, farmers' markets, and festivals that celebrate its vibrant culture. While Trenton faces economic challenges, its urban environment provides a dynamic setting with opportunities for cultural enrichment, dining, and entertainment.
