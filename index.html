Welcome to the Garden State!

New Jersey is home to over 9 million residents, making it one of the most densely populated states in the U.S. Despite its urban sprawl, it boasts diverse ecosystems, including the Pine Barrens, coastal shorelines, and lush farmland. A paradise of international culinary delights, New Jersey is one of America's greatest hidden gems.

Image courtesy of The Bowery Boys Foundation

A Brief History

New Jersey, originally inhabited by the Lenape Native Americans, was a region rich in natural resources and characterized by a diverse landscape. European settlement began in the early 17th century, with the Dutch and Swedes establishing the first colonies.

In 1664, the English took control, and New Jersey became a significant colony due to its fertile land and strategic location. The colony attracted a mix of settlers, including English Quakers, Scots, Irish, and Germans, who sought religious freedom and economic opportunities.

During the American Revolution, New Jersey played a crucial role as the "Crossroads of the Revolution," hosting numerous pivotal battles. Over time, it evolved into a densely populated and industrially robust state, known for its cultural diversity and economic significance.
